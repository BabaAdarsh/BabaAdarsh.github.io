# A reading list

Papers I keep coming back to — foundational and recent work that shaped how I think about prediction, learning, and the control of movement. Annotated with why each one matters.

## Internal models and adaptation

<div class="reading-item">
<a href="https://scholar.google.com/scholar?q=Shadmehr+Mussa-Ivaldi+1994+adaptive+representation+dynamics+motor+learning">Shadmehr & Mussa-Ivaldi (1994) — Adaptive representation of dynamics during learning of a motor task. <em>J Neurosci</em>.</a>
<p class="why">The experiment that launched modern motor adaptation research: reaching in a force field reveals the brain building an internal model of limb–environment dynamics.</p>
</div>

<div class="reading-item">
<a href="https://scholar.google.com/scholar?q=Wolpert+Ghahramani+Jordan+1995+internal+model+sensorimotor+integration">Wolpert, Ghahramani & Jordan (1995) — An internal model for sensorimotor integration. <em>Science</em>.</a>
<p class="why">Forward models made concrete: the brain predicts the sensory consequences of its own commands and combines prediction with feedback.</p>
</div>

<div class="reading-item">
<a href="https://scholar.google.com/scholar?q=Smith+Ghazizadeh+Shadmehr+2006+interacting+adaptive+processes+different+timescales">Smith, Ghazizadeh & Shadmehr (2006) — Interacting adaptive processes with different timescales underlie short-term motor learning. <em>PLOS Biology</em>.</a>
<p class="why">The two-state model: fast and slow learning processes jointly explain savings, interference, and spontaneous recovery. Still the workhorse model of the field.</p>
</div>

<div class="reading-item">
<a href="https://scholar.google.com/scholar?q=Herzfeld+Vaswani+Marko+Shadmehr+2014+memory+of+errors+sensorimotor+learning">Herzfeld, Vaswani, Marko & Shadmehr (2014) — A memory of errors in sensorimotor learning. <em>Science</em>.</a>
<p class="why">Learning rate is itself learned: the brain stores a history of past errors and adjusts how much to learn from new ones.</p>
</div>

## Context, inference, and memory

<div class="reading-item">
<a href="https://scholar.google.com/scholar?q=Heald+Lengyel+Wolpert+2021+contextual+inference+motor+learning+COIN">Heald, Lengyel & Wolpert (2021) — Contextual inference underlies the learning of sensorimotor repertoires. <em>Nature</em>.</a>
<p class="why">The COIN model: memory creation, expression, and updating all follow from Bayesian inference over latent contexts. Closest in spirit to my own work on competing memories.</p>
</div>

<div class="reading-item">
<a href="https://scholar.google.com/scholar?q=Kording+Wolpert+2004+Bayesian+integration+sensorimotor+learning">Körding & Wolpert (2004) — Bayesian integration in sensorimotor learning. <em>Nature</em>.</a>
<p class="why">Humans combine priors with noisy evidence near-optimally when reaching. The paper that made Bayesian frameworks unavoidable in movement science.</p>
</div>

<div class="reading-item">
<a href="https://scholar.google.com/scholar?q=Taylor+Krakauer+Ivry+2014+explicit+implicit+contributions+sensorimotor+adaptation">Taylor, Krakauer & Ivry (2014) — Explicit and implicit contributions to learning in a sensorimotor adaptation task. <em>J Neurosci</em>.</a>
<p class="why">Adaptation decomposed: deliberate aiming strategies and implicit recalibration are separable processes with very different signatures.</p>
</div>

## Control

<div class="reading-item">
<a href="https://scholar.google.com/scholar?q=Todorov+Jordan+2002+optimal+feedback+control+theory+motor+coordination">Todorov & Jordan (2002) — Optimal feedback control as a theory of motor coordination. <em>Nature Neuroscience</em>.</a>
<p class="why">Why movements are variable yet successful: the brain corrects only deviations that matter for the task. A unifying normative theory of control.</p>
</div>

<div class="reading-item">
<a href="https://scholar.google.com/scholar?q=Scott+2004+optimal+feedback+control+neural+basis+volitional+motor+control">Scott (2004) — Optimal feedback control and the neural basis of volitional motor control. <em>Nature Reviews Neuroscience</em>.</a>
<p class="why">Connects optimal feedback control to neural circuits — the framework guiding much of the work in our lab at Queen's.</p>
</div>

<div class="reading-item">
<a href="https://scholar.google.com/scholar?q=Pruszynski+Scott+2012+optimality+long-latency+reflex+review">Pruszynski & Scott (2012) — Optimal feedback control and the long-latency stretch response. <em>Exp Brain Res</em>.</a>
<p class="why">Rapid "reflex" responses are surprisingly intelligent — tuned to task goals within ~60 ms. Feedback control is where perception and action truly meet.</p>
</div>

## Learning systems, broadly

<div class="reading-item">
<a href="https://scholar.google.com/scholar?q=Daw+Niv+Dayan+2005+uncertainty+competition+prefrontal+dorsolateral+striatal+systems">Daw, Niv & Dayan (2005) — Uncertainty-based competition between prefrontal and dorsolateral striatal systems for behavioral control. <em>Nature Neuroscience</em>.</a>
<p class="why">Model-based vs model-free control as an uncertainty-weighted arbitration — a framework my recent work brings into motor learning.</p>
</div>

<div class="reading-item">
<a href="https://scholar.google.com/scholar?q=Krakauer+Hadjiosif+Xu+Wong+Haith+motor+learning+comprehensive+physiology+2019">Krakauer, Hadjiosif, Xu, Wong & Haith (2019) — Motor learning. <em>Comprehensive Physiology</em>.</a>
<p class="why">If you read one review on motor learning, read this. Opinionated, thorough, and honest about what we don't know.</p>
</div>
